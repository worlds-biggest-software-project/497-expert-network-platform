## Relevant Industry Standards or Protocols

- **CFA Institute Research Objectivity Standards** — govern expert network usage in investment research contexts; compliance programs are a buyer requirement
- **SEC Regulation FD (Fair Disclosure)** — prohibits selective disclosure of material non-public information; expert networks must enforce compliance guardrails
- **GDPR / CCPA** — governs expert profile data, consent for contact, and data retention
- **ISO 27001** — information security certification expected by enterprise buyers handling sensitive research conversations
- **Expert Network Compliance Programs (ENCP)** — industry-developed guidelines for managing insider risk in expert call arrangements

## Market Research

**Market Size:** The global expert network market reached approximately USD 3 billion in 2025, growing at roughly 12% annually, with projections exceeding USD 4.86 billion by end of 2026. Broader knowledge marketplace activity, including micro-consulting and digital advisory, adds significantly to this figure.

**Funding:** GLG has attracted significant private equity investment and has been valued at over USD 1 billion. AlphaSights and Guidepoint are both private and growing. The category has seen steady institutional investment given its high-margin, recurring revenue characteristics.

**Pricing Landscape:** Managed networks charge USD 500–3,000+ per expert call, plus annual subscription fees ranging from USD 50k–500k for enterprise clients. Self-serve platforms like CleverX aim for USD 200–800 per engagement. AI-native platforms are reducing the cost of sourcing while maintaining call quality.

**Key Buyer Personas:** Management consulting firms conducting rapid primary research; investment funds performing pre-investment diligence; corporate strategy teams benchmarking against industry peers; market research firms supplementing secondary data with primary expert perspective.

**Notable Trends:** AI-powered sourcing is compressing the time from research request to expert call from days to hours. Transcript libraries and searchable call archives are becoming a key value-add, reducing repeat sourcing costs. Compliance programs are evolving as regulators scrutinise material non-public information risks more closely. Platform self-serve models are democratising access beyond large consulting firms.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- AI-driven expert sourcing: NLP matches research briefs to expert profiles using semantic similarity, reducing manual recruiter effort for niche domains
- Automated compliance screening: AI checks expert profiles against restricted lists, insider risk flags, and compliance exclusion rules before engagement is offered
- Call summarisation and insight extraction: transcription and LLM summarisation convert expert calls into searchable, citable research artefacts immediately after each session
- Expert recommendation engine: analyses past call history and feedback to surface the highest-rated experts for similar future briefs without repeating manual search
- Knowledge gap identification: AI analyses a body of existing research and identifies the specific expert profiles and questions needed to fill critical gaps in a research programme
